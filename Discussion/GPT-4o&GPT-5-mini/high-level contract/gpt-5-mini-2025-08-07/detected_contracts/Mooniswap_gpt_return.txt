If the rescued token is not token0 or token1 (for example plain ETH when the pool tokens are ERC20s, or any other non-pool token), those post-transfer checks will still pass and the owner can withdraw ETH/other assets held by the contract. Although protected by onlyOwner, this allows the owner (or an attacker who obtains the owner key) to drain ETH or other non-pool tokens from the contract. Recommended mitigations: (1) forbid rescuing pool tokens and/or ETH (e.g. require(token != token0 && token != token1 && !token.isETH())), (2) perform validation before performing the transfer, or (3) restrict rescue to a governance-controlled or timelocked process and emit detailed events.
