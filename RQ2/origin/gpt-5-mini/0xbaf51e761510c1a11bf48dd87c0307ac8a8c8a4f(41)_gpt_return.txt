    "description": "The contract makes an external call to msg.sender (msg.sender.call.value(_am)()) before updating the caller's balance. Because call() forwards all remaining gas, a malicious recipient contract can re-enter CashOut (or another payable function) inside its fallback/receive, repeatedly invoking CashOut and draining funds while the original balance check still passes. The state change (balances[msg.sender] -= _am) occurs after the external interaction, violating the Checks-Effects-Interactions pattern. In Solidity ^0.4.19, call() does not provide a gas stipend and is unsafe for sending Ether without precautions. Recommended fixes: update the balance before making the external call (balances[msg.sender] -= _am), follow Checks-Effects-Interactions, or use a reentrancy guard (mutex) such as OpenZeppelin's ReentrancyGuard. Alternatively, use transfer()/send() (which forward limited gas) or implement a withdrawal/pull pattern where recipients withdraw funds from an updated internal accounting, and carefully handle any external logging calls (TransferLog.AddMessage) as they may also be external interactions."
