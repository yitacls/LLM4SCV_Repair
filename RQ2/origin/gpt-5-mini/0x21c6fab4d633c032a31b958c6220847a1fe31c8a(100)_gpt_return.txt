    "description": "The contract uses tx.origin for authorization (require(tx.origin == owner)). Relying on tx.origin to authenticate the caller is insecure: tx.origin is the original externally owned account that started the transaction, so a malicious contract can induce the owner (EOA) to call it and then have that contract call withdrawEther. In that scenario tx.origin will equal the owner (so the check passes) while msg.sender will be the malicious contract, allowing the attacker to cause the contract to transfer funds without the owner's direct intention. Impact: unauthorized withdrawal of Ether by tricking the owner into interacting with a malicious contract (phishing/cross-contract call attack). Recommended fix: use msg.sender for access control (e.g., require(msg.sender == owner)), or better, adopt a well-audited ownership pattern such as OpenZeppelin's Ownable and an onlyOwner modifier. Also ensure the owner variable is correctly declared as payable if needed (address vs address payable in newer compiler versions) and consider additional safeguards (multisig, timelocks) for high-value withdrawals."
