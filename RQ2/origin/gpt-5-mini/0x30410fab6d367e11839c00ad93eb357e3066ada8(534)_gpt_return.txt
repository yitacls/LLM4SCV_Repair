Relying on tx.origin for access control is insecure because tx.origin refers to the original externally owned account that started the transaction, not the immediate caller. An attacker can deploy a malicious contract that forwards calls to BetHorde; if the real owner interacts with the malicious contract, tx.origin will still be the owner while msg.sender will be the malicious contract, allowing the attacker-controlled contract to pass the check and execute owner-only functions. Impact: attacker can perform privileged operations (including draining funds or changing critical state) by tricking the owner into initiating a transaction with a malicious contract. Remediation: replace tx.origin checks with msg.sender (require(msg.sender == state.owner, \"Owner\")) or use a well-audited ownership pattern (e.g., OpenZeppelin Ownable).
