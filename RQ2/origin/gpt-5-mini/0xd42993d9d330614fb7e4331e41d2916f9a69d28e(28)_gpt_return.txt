Block timestamps are set by miners and can be biased within a certain range, so relying on now for critical time-sensitive logic (voting start/end) allows a miner or a colluding block producer to slightly advance or delay the recorded time to their advantage. Consequences include: shortening the voting window to prevent opposing votes, extending it to allow additional favorable votes, or otherwise manipulating end conditions that depend on timestamp. In Solidity ^0.4.11 this is a known issue — block.timestamp should not be treated as an authoritative source for exact timing. Safer approaches include using block.number-based durations (measured in blocks) or adding tolerances and checks (e.g., minimum/maximum allowed durations, or external oracle/time-lock patterns) instead of directly trusting now for critical voting deadlines."
