    "description": "The contract uses tx.origin for authorization (require(tx.origin == owner())). Relying on tx.origin is insecure: an attacker can deploy a malicious contract and trick the owner into interacting with it (phishing). If the owner (EOA) calls the malicious contract, and that contract forwards a call to addMeByRC, tx.origin will still equal the owner's address so the require passes, while msg.sender will be the attacker's contract address. As a result the attacker's contract becomes marked in rc (rc[msg.sender] = true) and gains whatever privileges are granted to RCs. Impact: unauthorized role assignment, privilege escalation, and potential theft or unauthorized actions. Fix: do not use tx.origin for access control. Use require(msg.sender == owner()) or the standard onlyOwner modifier (Ownable) and implement explicit role-management checks. Also consider defense-in-depth (e.g., multisig for owner actions, whitelist verification) to prevent social-engineering attacks."
