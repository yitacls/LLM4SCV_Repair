  "description": "The contract performs external calls to Mooniswap pools and token contracts (withdraw, swaps, token transfers, and ETH transfer to tx.origin) before completing and updating its internal accounting. This violates the checks-effects-interactions pattern and allows a malicious or compromised external contract (a Mooniswap pool or token implementing callbacks) to re-enter ReferralFeeReceiver and call privileged functions such as updateReward or other entry points. Because the contract updates important state (epoch balances, token.currentEpoch, user epoch pointers and balances) only after these external interactions, a reentrant call can manipulate state or claim funds multiple times.\n\nPossible exploitation scenario:\n- An attacker controls (or supplies) a malicious pool/token in the swap/withdraw path. When ReferralFeeReceiver calls mooniswap.withdraw(...) or _swap(...), the malicious contract's fallback/logic re-enters ReferralFeeReceiver (for example, calling updateReward or claim functions). Because the contract has not yet updated epoch balances or advanced epochs, the reentrant call can cause inconsistent accounting allowing the attacker to increase their share, double-claim inch tokens, or cause other users' balances to be misallocated or drained.\n\nSpecific dangerous patterns observed:\n- External call (withdraw/_swap/transfer) occurs before updating epochBalance.* fields and token.currentEpoch.\n- External ETH transfer via tx.origin.transfer is used (also discouraged), and it happens before adjustments of accounting for that epoch.\n\nRecommended mitigations:\n- Follow checks-effects-interactions: update all relevant contract state (epoch balances, currentEpoch, user markers) BEFORE performing external calls.\n- Introduce a nonReentrant guard (e.g., OpenZeppelin ReentrancyGuard) on functions that perform external calls and modify state.\n- Avoid using tx.origin for transfers; use msg.sender or explicit recipient addresses, and ensure ETH/token transfers are made after internal state updates.\n- Minimize trust in external pools/tokens; treat external calls as potentially reentrant and defensive-program accordingly (validate invariants after calls, or move interactions to the end of functions).\n\nWithout these fixes, an attacker can exploit reentrancy to corrupt epoch accounting and steal or double-claim referral rewards."
