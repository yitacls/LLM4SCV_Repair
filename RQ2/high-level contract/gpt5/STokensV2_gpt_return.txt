Because _calculatePendingRewards is invoked in hot paths (e.g., transfer hooks via _beforeTokenTransfer, calculateRewards, and calculateHolderRewards), the gas cost of common operations (transfers, reward calculations, mint/burn flows) grows linearly with the number of stored reward-change entries. If the arrays become sufficiently large (whether due to many legitimate admin updates or a malicious/compromised admin repeatedly calling setRewardRate), executing _calculatePendingRewards can exceed the block gas limit and revert. This causes a denial-of-service: transfers and reward-related operations will fail, effectively freezing token activity.\n\nNotes on mitigations: the vulnerability is exacerbated because the loop is unbounded and executed during transfer hooks. Although setRewardRate is restricted to DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE, an attacker who gains that role or an admin acting maliciously can exploit this to create a DoS. Recommended mitigations include: avoid unbounded historical iteration in on-chain hot paths (store cumulative accounting to allow O(1) computations), limit or cap history length, process history in bounded chunks with checkpointing, or move history traversal off-chain and store per-user checkpoints that don't require scanning the full reward history during transfers."
