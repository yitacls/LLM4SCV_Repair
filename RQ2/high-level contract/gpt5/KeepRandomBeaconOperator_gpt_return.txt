If previousEntry is predictable, replayable, or can be supplied/controlled by an authorized service contract or an adversary cooperating with a service contract, the group selection can be biased or manipulated. An attacker who can choose or influence previousEntry (or replay earlier entries) may influence which group is chosen, enabling preferential selection of groups they control or can collude with. That can lead to predictable/biased group selection, enabling attacks such as suppression or control of future beacon outputs, extraction of rewards, or aiding malicious signature submissions.\n\n2) Use of block.number to compute reward delayFactor (timing-dependent economic incentives):\n   - getDelayFactor uses block.number to compute entryReceivedBlock, remainingBlocks, and ultimately delayFactor which directly affects groupMemberReward, submitterReward and subsidy. Miners and block proposers have some control over whether and when transactions are included in a block (and can delay inclusion or withhold blocks), which can change block.number at the time of execution and therefore change the computed delayFactor and resulting rewards. Although block.number is less directly manipulable than timestamp, miners can still influence the effective reward distribution by deciding when to include or reorder transactions. This timing-based manipulation can be exploited to increase the submitter reward or decrease group member rewards by causing transactions to be mined in blocks that yield more favorable delayFactor.\n\nImpact:\n   - Biased group selection undermines randomness guarantees and can allow an attacker to repeatedly get groups composed of malicious or colluding members, enabling compromised beacon outputs or extraction of funds/rewards.\n   - Miner/attacker manipulation of block-number-based reward calculations can skew incentives, resulting in unfair or exploitable payouts, and may enable miners to profit by selectively including or excluding transactions.\n\nRecommendations:\n   - Do not rely solely on previousEntry supplied externally or easily predictable chain data as a randomness/selection seed.
