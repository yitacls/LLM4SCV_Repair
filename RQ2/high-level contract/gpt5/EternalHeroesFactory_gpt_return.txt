  "description": "The contract issues an external call to arcadeumCoin.safeTransferFrom(...) to refund ARC tokens to the recipient before performing the internal state-changing action factoryManager.batchMint(...). During the safeTransferFrom call the ERC-1155 token contract will invoke the recipient's onERC1155Received callback (if the recipient is a contract). A malicious recipient contract can therefore reenter EternalHeroesFactory (for example by triggering a new ARC transfer to this factory or calling functions exposed by the factory) while the original purchase execution has not yet completed. Because batchMint (which updates supply and finalizes the purchase) is executed only after the refund, reentrancy can lead to inconsistent state or unintended behavior such as double-minting, bypassing price/tier checks, or manipulation of subsequent purchases. \n\nPotential exploit scenario:\n- Attacker submits a purchase encoded in _data such that refundAmount > 0 and sets the recipient to a malicious contract.\n- When EternalHeroesFactory calls arcadeumCoin.safeTransferFrom(..., recipient, ...), the ERC-1155 token contract invokes the malicious recipient's onERC1155Received.\n- The malicious onERC1155Received reenters EternalHeroesFactory (for example by transferring ARC to the factory or calling a purchase flow) while the original purchase is still mid-execution (supply not yet updated, minting not yet performed).\n- The attacker can exploit the inconsistent state to obtain more tokens than allowed, cause erroneous refunds, or otherwise manipulate the factory logic.\n\nRecommended mitigations:\n- Follow Checks-Effects-Interactions: perform all internal state updates (or call factoryManager.batchMint) before making external calls that can invoke untrusted contracts.\n- Move the refund (external transfer) to after batchMint or change refunds to a pull pattern: record refund balances in a mapping and let users withdraw refunds via an explicit withdraw function (pull over push).\n- Add a reentrancy guard (e.g., OpenZeppelin ReentrancyGuard) to _buy (and other external entry points) to prevent reentrant calls during execution.\n- Minimize the amount
